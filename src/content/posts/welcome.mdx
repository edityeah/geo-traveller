For a long time this blog has lived on WordPress, and for a long time I've been
mostly fighting the tools instead of writing. Themes breaking after updates,
plugins demanding attention, pages loading slowly on shared hosting — every
visit to the admin felt like maintenance, not writing.

So Geo-Traveller has a new home now. The site you're reading is a static site,
generated from posts I write in Notion. There is no database, no plugins, no
admin login. When I publish a new post, the site rebuilds itself in a couple of
minutes and goes live, served from a CDN. It loads instantly, costs almost
nothing, and asks nothing of me except to write.

## Why the change

The old site wasn't broken — it just kept getting in the way. WordPress is a
remarkable piece of software, but it carries the weight of every site it's ever
powered. For a one-person travel journal, that weight became the project itself.

I wanted the tools to fade out of the way. To open Notion, write a post the way
I write everything else, hit publish, and be done.

## What's the same

Every post that lived on the old site lives here too. The URLs of the old posts
still work — anything that moved gets a redirect. RSS feeds still work. Search
engines still see the same content.

## What's next

I'll be re-publishing migrated posts in batches over the next few weeks, and
starting fresh writing soon. If you've been a reader through any era of this
site — thank you. There's more coming.
