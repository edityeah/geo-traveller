# Uttar Pradesh's Pioneering Move: Bundelkhand Expressway to Transform into a Solar-Powered Marvel, Aiming for 50 MW Power Generation

In a groundbreaking initiative, the Uttar Pradesh government is transforming the Bundelkhand Expressway into the state's inaugural solar-powered expressway. This ambitious project is a part of Uttar Pradesh's strategy to boost solar energy utilization, setting a precedent in renewable energy efforts.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8191-a6e4-dc1090b651b4 type:heading_1 -->
# Revolutionizing Roadways with Solar Energy: The Bundelkhand Expressway Project

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-812e-bb6a-cc04461eae32 type:image -->
![Revolutionizing Roadways with Solar Energy: The Bundelkhand Expressway Project](https://geo-traveller.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/image-19-1024x683.png)

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81ac-9e4d-defbfea33da0 type:paragraph -->
The government, embracing the Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model, is set to establish solar power plants alongside the Bundelkhand Expressway. These plants will boast an impressive capacity of 550 MW. Approximately 1700 hectares of land have been allocated for this eco-friendly endeavor, attracting interest from various renowned corporations.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d3-b2e8-c2f95e675ec3 type:paragraph -->
Projected to benefit around one lakh households linked to the expressway, this solar venture is not just an energy solution but a community upliftment project. With an operational lifespan of 25 years and an expected return on investment within 10 to 12 years, the project is as economically viable as it is environmentally friendly.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8164-b9ab-c5e76814eed6 type:paragraph -->
Managed by the Uttar Pradesh Expressway Industrial Development Authority (UPEIDA), the project has already completed its Expression of Interest (EOI) phase and a thorough Due Diligence Study as of August 2023. The bidding process, following the near completion of the Request for Proposals (RFP) for developer selection, is anticipated to commence shortly.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8187-934e-c9c1fa75b186 type:paragraph -->
In October 2023, prominent solar power developers like Tuscon Ltd., Torrent Power Ltd., and Somaya Solar Solutions Pvt. Ltd., among others, showcased their proposals, signaling robust industry participation.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-813c-af79-fae41a82de4e type:heading_1 -->
# A Catalyst for E-Mobility and Sustainable Development

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-814a-a8be-ca7918a6634d type:paragraph -->
Upon completion, the solar-powered expressway is expected to not only meet energy requirements but also pave the way for e-mobility and regional development. The project is set to significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to the fight against climate change.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81dc-baed-cf2b07e3d73c type:paragraph -->
The initiative promises to diversify energy sources, enhancing grid access and fostering economic growth. Job creation is another positive outcome, with local employment opportunities expected to arise. Additionally, the generated electricity will benefit surrounding villages, marking a step towards inclusive development.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-813b-8fc5-c2ad9482a060 type:heading_1 -->
# Meeting Diverse Energy Needs

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-813b-9e3b-f0f01e063bc3 type:paragraph -->
The solar-powered Bundelkhand Expressway is also envisioned to support various energy needs, including the charging of electric vehicles. UPEIDA stands to gain significantly from this project. Lease rent from the expressway could generate approximately ₹4 crores for UPEIDA, with an annual energy sales profit projected at around ₹50 crore. Moreover, the replication of this model on other expressways like Purvanchal, Agra-Lucknow, and Gorakhpur could reduce UPEIDA's energy expenses by ₹6 crore annually.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81a4-b547-f879d35932b8 type:heading_1 -->
# Harnessing the Geographical Advantage

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-810a-bae5-efb65e212f9f type:paragraph -->
The Bundelkhand Expressway is an ideal location for solar power plants, thanks to its easy land access and dry, arid climate, complemented by an average annual rainfall of 800-900 mm and ample sunlight. This 296 km-long, four-lane expressway, with its main carriageway and service lane, also includes a vacant stretch ideal for solar panel installation. This unique feature is set to revolutionize the expressway into a fully solar-powered highway, marking a historical transition in sustainable infrastructure development.
