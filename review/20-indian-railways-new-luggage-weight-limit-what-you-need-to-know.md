# Understanding Indian Railways' Updated Luggage Regulations

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81a1-9f16-d3c4d0995509 type:paragraph -->
Every day, a significant number of passengers rely on Indian Railways for their travel needs. In the past, travelers could carry a substantial amount of luggage on trains. However, Indian Railways has now implemented weight limits, varying depending on the class of travel.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8151-b015-c20a0e4146da type:image -->
![image](https://geo-traveller.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/image-6.png)

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81da-b2f1-fc2a6fe28977 type:paragraph -->
Passengers with tickets for sleeper coaches are allowed a maximum luggage weight of 40 kg, as reported by Informal Newz. For those traveling in AC two-tier coaches, the allowance is increased to 50 kg. The most generous weight allowance is granted to AC first-class passengers, who can carry up to 70 kg of luggage. Understanding these limits will help you plan your trip and book tickets accordingly.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-815e-86bf-ecdae8b05832 type:paragraph -->
Gone are the days when passengers could bring excessive baggage on trains. This new regulation aligns Indian Railways with airlines, where specific weight limits are set for passengers in different classes.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81b3-a2e2-de46bee59ea5 type:heading_1 -->
# Penalties for Exceeding Luggage Limits

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81e2-b7d5-efa179804ca0 type:table -->
[table block — see Notion for content]

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81a8-9d04-d98e8c718c35 type:paragraph -->
Indian Railways not only imposes luggage weight limits but also enforces penalties for non-compliance. Passengers who exceed the specified luggage allowance may be subject to fines. Informal Newz reports that Indian Railways may charge a fine if travelers fail to adhere to the guidelines.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-819d-958f-f8e58871f4ba type:paragraph -->
The fine for carrying excess luggage is set at ₹654, with potential variations based on the total distance of the train journey. If a passenger is traveling within 500 km and their luggage exceeds 40 kg, they may incur a fine of ₹109 for failing to comply with the new luggage weight rule.
