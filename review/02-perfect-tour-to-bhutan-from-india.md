Popularly known as "the land of thunder dragons", Bhutan is a beautiful country between China and India. It is a very small Kingdom in the Himalayas and consists of only 20 districts called the Dzongkhags. Bhutan is the first country globally with specific constitutional obligations on its people to protect the environment. Among its requirements: At least 60% of the nation must remain under forest cover. The salubrious climate, breathtaking views, and majestic monasteries may seem cliched to describe Bhutan's beauty, but each word used for this gorgeous country is in its purest form. This is a complete Bhutan Travel Blog for all those planning a trip to this beautiful country. So, how to travel to Bhutan from India? How to plan a perfect tour of Bhutan from India? Being our first international trip, we got into planning around 6 months in advance. The first thing on our list was to decide how we would reach Bhutan from Mumbai. There are three options to reach Bhutan:

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-814c-a989-fa6bcc18902c type:bulleted_list_item -->
- By Air

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-818e-8cf6-c1fc6473ff65 type:paragraph -->
Flights from India to Bhutan Two direct airlines, namely, Drukair and Bhutan Airlines, operate flights from Delhi, Gaya, Bagdogra, Kolkata, Mumbai and Guwahati to Paro, Bhutan's only international airport. (Indian travellers can be offered special fares in Drukair if the airline is contacted through email.)

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d7-9b9d-ca1e73a12eb2 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- By Rail

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-812c-a755-d97adb82e478 type:paragraph -->
This is one of the cheapest ways to reach Bhutan from India. Hasimara is the nearest railway station to Jaigaon, an Indian town on Indo-Bhutan Border. It is situated at a distance of 17 km from Jaigaon. There are trains to Hasimara from Kolkata (690 km), Delhi (1663 km), Ranchi (749 km), and Kanpur (1197 km). Another station that offers decent connectivity is situated in New Jalpaiguri. The tickets and timetable are available on Indian Railway's IRCTC Website. Though trains take longer but are relatively cost-efficient and are ideal for budget travellers. (Our budgeted travel route was a train from Mumbai to Kolkata, followed by a train journey in the evening from Seldah to Hasimara and getting a local rickshaw via Jaigaon - Phuentsholing road route to Phuentsholing.)

3 ways of entering Bhutan from India by Road There are 3 points to cross the India-Bhutan Border viz Gelephu, Samdrup Jongkhar and Phuentsholing. While Gelephu and Samdrup Jongkhar are connected to the Indian State of Assam, Phuentsholing is accessible from the nearby Indian state of West Bengal. The most common way to enter Bhutan from India by road is from Jaigaon town in West Bengal. It is only 4.3 km from the border town of Bhutan, Phuentsholing. Private cabs are available between the two places. People getting down at Bagdogra (by flight) or New Jalpaiguri (by train) can also avail of cab services. The airport at Bagdogra has prepaid taxis as well.

# Do we need Visa to enter Bhutan?

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d3-8ffd-c71aec582af2 type:paragraph -->
No! Visa is not required for Indian citizens. A passport or Voter ID is sufficient at the crossing points to get the entry permit. Without the permit, you are not allowed to leave any crossing points. You must also carry 3-4 passport-sized photos to fill out the permit form.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-812a-b1df-cf154680962c type:heading_1 -->
# How to Get Tourist Permit in Bhutan?

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d9-9122-cf3bc56ef467 type:image -->

Regional Immigration Office, Phuentsholing Bhutan The Immigration Office in Phuentsholing issues permits (for SAARC nations). A few documents need to be verified before the tourist permit is handed out. Those travellers flying directly to Paro can get their permits from Paro International Airport.

# The ideal time to visit Bhutan

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-812c-b395-fa4d7e255f85 type:paragraph -->
October to December is the ideal time to visit Bhutan as the air is clean and fresh with sunny skies. January and February are colder, but from then until April, the climate remains dry and pleasant.

# Things to consider

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-813f-916b-d3441c424241 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Travellers coming via flight can obtain the permit on all days through the Immigration Office at Paro International airport upon arrival.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81e0-9d82-cc03e72abfd1 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- A prior hotel booking at all places proposed to stay in Bhutan is advisable for all Indian visitors, as the RGoB Immigration authorities could ask for it;

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8148-ab89-cdd714513c00 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Indian nationals coming to Bhutan via the road route must obtain an ‘Entry Permit’ based on a valid Travel Document from the Immigration Office of RGoB at Phuntsholing, located just ahead of the Indo -Bhutan border gate opposite Jaigaon, West Bengal, India.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8116-9f6f-dc9de03b1653 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- The Phuntsholing Regional Immigration Office remains typically open from 6 AM to 8 PM on all working days and remains closed on Saturdays / Sundays and other local holidays. Hence, you should check the date of arrival accordingly in case you are entering Bhutan via Jaigaon - Phuntsholing road route.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8155-8ea8-f6d92032ec45 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Once the permit is issued, you can use the same to buy a local SIM card since none of the Indian Sim cards works once you cross the border.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-810e-b9c8-d7b1b581719a type:paragraph -->
As per the latest update, tourists from India need to pay 1200/day as part of the Sustainable development fee, which is applicable from 2020.

Our stay in Thimphu! You may find many budgeted stay options online on booking.com, Makemytrip, Airbnb etc., starting from 2000-8000/day, depending on luxuries. From Phuentsholing to the capital city, Thimphu, you can find hotels within your budget everywhere. Here are a few things that you should know while booking hotels in Bhutan:

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-811c-af71-d205fec33ff9 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- An average hotel during the peak season can cost up to INR 2500, whereas the same hotel offers rooms at INR 1300-1500 during the offseason. There are also accommodations available on a shoestring budget (INR 400-500), but they will be pretty basic and may lack comfort amenities.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81ca-bab2-d559897a3439 type:bulleted_list_item -->

# Punakha

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-816d-90cb-ed5cda8167f8 type:paragraph -->
Punakha, located 85 km east of Thimphu, is a beautiful valley known for its Dzong/Fortress, situated at the confluence of two famous rivers of Bhutan, Mo and Pho. The gorgeous and majestic dzong of Punakha is historically significant. It was not only the centre/capital of Bhutan (between 1637 and 1907) but is also home to the sacred relic Rangjung Kharsapani. The valley and the dzong both are popular tourist places in Bhutan. The place has a lot of visitors (both locals and tourists) during the Punakha Tshechu and Drubchen.

# Paro

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81c0-b429-e3be8b502067 type:image -->
![image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d4/Rinpung_Dzong%2C_Bhutan_18.jpg)

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8136-91b1-f0e7ee9417e5 type:paragraph -->
Paro Dzong Paro is a picturesque place that can make you fall in love with its first glimpse and has one of the widest valleys in Bhutan. Spanning from the confluence of the Paro and Wang rivers at Chuzom up to Mt. Jomolhari at the Tibetan border to the North, this heavenly place is where you can think of settling down. The main Paro town is nothing but a small, well-planned area with houses, hotels, shops and restaurants all in a perfect equation in the broadest part of the valley. At the same time, its main tourist attractions like the Rinpung Dzong, National Museum and the very popular Taktsang Monastery/Tiger’s Nest Monastery are nestled in the hills.

May 2018 is when we set out with a plan to have one International stamp on our passports with a budgeted approach, and that's how we landed in Bhutan. Since we started our journey from Mumbai, we also got a day and a half to explore Kolkata. Boarding a train and reaching the border in a blink of an eye, finding ourselves on the other side of the road felt terrific. Trust me; you can count the difference by appearance and how beautiful this country is. No traffic signal and a disciplined approach to crossing the road define punctuality in Bhutan. People in Bhutan were friendly, and the weather kept changing throughout the day. Honestly, those five days were the best time we could even ask for or can even compare. From shoot mode to eating the spicy yet delicious food, hiking, walking and visiting peaceful and colourful monasteries is how we sum up our trip.
