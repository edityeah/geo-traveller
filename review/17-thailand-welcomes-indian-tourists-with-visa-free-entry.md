# Thailand Welcomes Indian Tourists with Visa-Free Entry

**Date:** 2023-11-04  
**Slug:** thailand-welcomes-indian-tourists-with-visa-free-entry  
**Original:** https://geo-traveller.com/thailand-welcomes-indian-tourists-with-visa-free-entry/  
**Excerpt:**   
**Blocks:** 14

---

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81a5-bb2f-f83a8cb6d7f5 type:paragraph -->
To revitalize its tourism sector, Thailand has rolled out a visa-free entry policy for Indian travelers. This initiative, effective from November 10, 2023, to May 10, 2024, is set to bolster the influx of tourists from India, offering them a hassle-free travel experience.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8156-9a9c-f4a4812aba69 type:heading_1 -->
# Why This Matters?

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81ee-93f1-fdca7a3c7a43 type:paragraph -->
The visa exemption presents a significant opportunity for Indian globetrotters. Not only does it eliminate the visa application process, but it also translates into substantial savings. Typically, a Thai visa costs around 2,200 baht, which is approximately INR 5,100. Now, travelers can reallocate these funds to enhance their travel experience in the vibrant landscapes of Thailand.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81fe-929b-cbc39e9c5d06 type:heading_1 -->
# The 30-Day Window

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8198-a2ac-d972930a3460 type:paragraph -->
Under this new policy, Indian tourists can enjoy up to 30 days in Thailand without a visa. This period is ideal for exploring Thailand's rich cultural tapestry, indulging in its world-renowned cuisine, and experiencing the warmth of Thai hospitality.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8154-bcb3-f1396547203e type:heading_1 -->
# A Closer Look at the Visa-Free Countries for Indians

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-811e-8fe3-e7c2a066f9ed type:paragraph -->
Apart from Thailand, Indian passport holders can traverse several countries without the need for a pre-arranged visa. Here's an updated list of destinations where Indians can travel visa-free or with visa-on-arrival access:

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81f3-9c40-ed8ad9b5a9d7 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Asia: Bhutan, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and now, Thailand.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8126-89ed-f3e3bc6c6a5a type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Caribbean: Barbados, Dominica, Grenada, Haiti, Jamaica, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d9-b2d6-c0559f937bcd type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Oceania: Fiji, Vanuatu, Micronesia.

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8107-af3c-d74befc84b11 type:bulleted_list_item -->
- Africa: Gabon, Gambia, Mauritius, Senegal, Seychelles, Eswatini (formerly Swaziland).

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8145-a60b-cf84aa89582c type:heading_1 -->
# Enhancing the Travel Experience

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81de-b558-e89f152d5a90 type:paragraph -->
With visa-free access, Indian tourists can look forward to an enriched travel experience. Thailand's diverse offerings, from the bustling streets of Bangkok to the serene beaches of Phuket, become readily accessible. Moreover, the cost savings can be redirected towards experiences like island hopping, spa retreats, or culinary tours.
