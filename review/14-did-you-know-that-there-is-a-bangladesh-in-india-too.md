## A Hidden Gem of a place in Kashmir called Bangladesh

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81c7-b12e-c8d76619b5e0 type:paragraph -->
Located approximately 30 kilometers away from the Bandipora district of Kashmir, the village of Bangladesh sits near the enchanting Wular Lake. With a population of around 300 people, this picturesque village is adorned with waterfalls, mountains, and a tranquil ambiance that captivates visitors. The proximity to Wular Lake adds to its charm, providing a scenic backdrop and offering a range of activities such as fishing, boating, and birdwatching.

## Origin of the Name

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-819a-84c8-c2dc49324104 type:paragraph -->
The naming of Bangladesh village is an intriguing story. After the formation of the country of Bangladesh in 1971, the village was originally called Jurimon. However, tragedy struck when the village was set ablaze, leaving its citizens homeless. Determined to rebuild their lives, the villagers resettled a short distance away from the burnt village and named it Bangladesh as a symbol of resilience and hope.

## Growth and Recognition

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-81d1-bc44-d74efc9cad74 type:paragraph -->
What began as a small settlement with just a handful of homes has now grown to encompass 50 to 60 houses. The villagers primarily depend on agriculture for their livelihood, embracing the fertile land surrounding their village. Despite their struggles, the village of Bangladesh remained unrecognized by government records for a long time. However, in 2010, the DC office in Bandipora officially recognized Bangladesh as a separate village, acknowledging its existence and the needs of its inhabitants.

## Promoting Sustainable Tourism

<!-- block:37a7bc30-b890-8142-94fc-c971b534a76c type:paragraph -->
With the aim of boosting tourism in the region, the government is committed to providing better facilities and support to the villagers of Bangladesh. Recognizing the potential of this hidden gem, officials are taking steps to promote sustainable tourism in the area. This includes improving infrastructure, creating employment opportunities, and enhancing the overall visitor experience. By doing so, the government hopes to not only attract tourists but also empower the local community, enabling them to generate more income and improve their standard of living.
